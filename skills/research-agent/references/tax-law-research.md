# Tax Law Research

## Start With Scope

Always capture:

- jurisdiction
- tax year
- taxpayer type
- transaction type
- filing form or schedule

Tax answers are often wrong because one of those dimensions changed.

## Authority Order

For United States federal tax topics, prefer this order:

1. Internal Revenue Code or other enacted law
2. Treasury regulations
3. Internal Revenue Bulletin guidance such as revenue rulings, revenue procedures, notices, and treasury decisions
4. IRS forms, instructions, publications, and official webpages
5. FAQs or secondary commentary

Use lower-authority material to explain administration, not to overrule higher-authority text.

## Reliability Notes

- IRS guidance published in the Internal Revenue Bulletin has precedential value.
- IRS forms, instructions, publications, and FAQs are useful, but they are not the same authority level as the Code, regulations, or Bulletin guidance.
- Keep exact review dates because tax guidance changes frequently.

## Output Requirements

Every tax research result should state:

- the question answered
- the tax year and jurisdiction
- the authority level of each key source
- the practical conclusion
- the unresolved edge cases

## Safety Boundary

Do not present tax research as personalized legal advice. If the issue is ambiguous, high-dollar, multi-jurisdictional, or penalty-sensitive, mark it for professional review.
